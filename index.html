VISION

WOOFENOMICS began as a social experiment about burning tokens, but was late to join the memecoin bandwagon. Early on we’ve shifted towards building something that is largely missing in the Solana ecosystem, which is a central marketplace for NFTs to be auctioned, sold and put on display. NFTs should be able to be safely stored while they are not on the market, but that doesn’t mean others cannot see them or place value on them in the meantime. The WOOF NFT Marketplace is one that is social, dynamic and cross-chain - but we are adding in a final component that is missing entirely from the crypto/NFT space. With HOWL Finance, it will be possible to create collateralized loans to purchase NFTs that are just out of your budget. The marketplace and HOWL NFT loan protocol will launch this summer

ABOUT

WOOFENOMICS is a community driven project that begins with a simple deflationary model, but relies on active participation by WOOFers to guide direction and strategy. The largest request by WOOF holders is to burn the expansive supply. Following the unheld token burns, the question remains what will become of the project? This period leading up to the incineration of unheld supply will help the project launch a governance structure, NFT marketplace and a slate of many other community holder events. We support the memecoin movement but also seek to provide value to holders in other ways such as collectible assets, collaborations with other Solana Ecosystem projects and whatever else the community decides.

DECREASING SUPPLY

What about a token with a decreasing supply? A consistent burn model that has not been applied before to such extreme levels is an exponential deflationary model. Cryptocurrency has unlocked the potential to test new economic models and put them in action.

50% BURN EACH DAY

Starting at 1 trillion total supply, each day 50% of supply will be burned. In less than 40 days, the available supply would drop to 0. With nearly 20 billion tokens actively circulating, the market cap will no doubt grow.
